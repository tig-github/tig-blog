Last year I had the fortune of visiting Montreal with my mom in early September, and I loved it. Montreal is a beautiful city with a rich history, delicious food from many cuisines, amazing neighborhoods, and many things to do. I spent a little over a week in the city, spending time in Downtown, Vieux Montreal, Le Plateau-Mont-Royal, Little Italy, Notre Dame de Grace, Snowdon, and more. Join me as I go over some of my favorite places, restaurants, and things to do in the city.\
&nbsp;

On the first day, along with spending time with family, I visited Vieux Port along the St Laurence River. It is a lovely waterfront along the city with nice activities like ziplining and a huge Ferris wheel. It is a very touristy area, but it was still a nice place to spend an afternoon walking around. One thing I really appreciated about the city was how alive it felt - everywhere I went there were always people walking, relaxing, and overall enjoying the city we were in. Montreal in the summer/early fall is a special place.\
&nbsp;

There was even a taco festival going on, and as someone from Southern California I was too curious not to go. It was an upbeat party vibe with plenty of Mexican food options (and a Spanish paella stand), overall a good time. I got some barbacoa tacos, and they were pretty good. I was pleasantly surprised to get them all the way up in Canada.\
&nbsp;

![[IMG_9400 Cropped.png]][600][400][Old Port Montreal along the Saint Laurence river]\
&nbsp;

Food might have been my favorite part of the whole trip! I got to try famous Montreal smoked meat from the famous Schwartz Deli, a popular and touristy deli in Le Plateau-Mont-Royal. The smoked meat had a delicious flavor and came with generous portions, and I really enjoyed the poutine as well. I also tried Snowdon Deli, and enjoyed it quite a lot - I will have to say I liked Schwartz a bit more. I enjoyed delicious bagels at Fairmount Bagel, thinner than I'm used to but absolutely delightful.\
&nbsp;

![[IMG_9452 Cropped.png]][600][400][Schwartz Deli smoked meat sandwich (delicious)]\
&nbsp;

My favorite meals were at the North African restaurants I went to. As an Algerian it was welcome to see a lot of Algerian businesses and restaurants around the city, and I had a wonderful meal at Khaymat El-Hodna. I loved their bourek and lamb, but the highlight was the incredible zviti. I also got to eat at the Tunisian restaurant El Mida, and enjoyed wonderful merguez, mechouia, and got to try the divisive mloukhia. I really like mloukhia! I also want to shoutout the shawarma wrap at Amir. The place has middle of the road reviews, but it was the best shawarma I have had in years and I still think about it months later. There is a lot of other food I want to try next time I visit, like the Portuguese restaurant Coco Rico, poutine at a dedicated spot, and Mauritanian and Senegalese cuisine.\
&nbsp;

![[IMG_9518.jpg]][600][400][Beautiful building in Downtown Montreal]\
&nbsp;

I got to spend a good amount of time exploring Downtown and Vieux Montreal. I found Downtown to be interesting and enjoyable to walk through, with a lot of interesting churches scattered about. My favorite was the Mary, Queen of the World Cathedral; a replica of St Peter's in Rome. It was a beautiful (and free!) experience. I also enjoyed the underground city, it felt a bit surreal to traverse such large areas entirely in tunnels. The metro also impressed me with its cleanliness and efficiency.\
&nbsp;

Vieux Montreal was cool too. I did enjoy the Notre Dame Basilica of Montreal, though it is expensive to go inside and was under construction while I was there. The cobblestone streets and old architecture are nice, but this side of the city is highly touristy so it wasn't my favorite. I do think I would like to spend more time a bit deeper in the old city to get a better feel for it.\
&nbsp;

![[IMG_9445 Cropped.png]][600][400][Mount Royal park during sunset]\
&nbsp;

The center of the city features a huge park called Mont Royal with delightful views of Montreal. The park is lovely and a great place to have a picnic on a summer evening - though be warned that countless yellow jackets will swarm you if you hold up a yellow soda. I learned the hard way. I also loved visiting the Oratory just south of the park. The Oratory is the largest church in all of Canada, towering over the hill its built on. The inside is full of chapels and beautiful architecture and art. The views from the top are also nice, showing much of the southern neighborhoods of Montreal.\
&nbsp;

![[Mont Royal and the Oratory.png]][800][400][A view of Montreal from Mount Royal park]\
&nbsp;

I got to explore many other neighborhoods during my time on the island. I enjoyed the large farmers market and liveliness of Little Italy, and would like to go back and spend more time in the area. I spent a lot of time in Notre-Dame-De-Grâce, a quieter Anglophone neighborhood southwest of downtown. It was actually where I had my first coffee in 7 weeks after I had previously quit (and since then I have been drinking it lol). I enjoyed the architecture and found the people to be very friendly, it was a place I could easily see myself living in.\
&nbsp;

My favorite neighborhood was Plateau Mont-Royal. I loved the feel of the neighborhood, the liveliness, the great food (such as Schwartz), and the proximity to much of the city. I did not spend much time there and would love to go back and spend a day or two just roaming the streets and eating all the delicious food.\
&nbsp;

And lastly I want to shout out the Biodome, a very unique indoor zoo located north of downtown. The Biodome simulates various different environments (Arctic, Jungle, Temperate Forest, etc.) and hosts animals from the respective areas. It was a super cool and fun zoo to explore, and easy to navigate. If you are going with family it is a great place for kids to see.\
&nbsp;

![[IMG_9417 1.jpg]][600][400][The markets in Little Italy]\
&nbsp;

In conclusion Montreal exceeded my high expectations. I loved the culture, architecture, food, atmosphere, and living breathing feeling of the city. I also understand that it helped that I went in the summer, but it is magical how the whole city comes alive at that time of the year. I would love to go back and eat all the delicious food I missed out on, and rent a car to drive up to Quebec City, grabbing some poutine on the way up.
